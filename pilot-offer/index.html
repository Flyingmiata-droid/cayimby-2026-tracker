What you get every week

A 30–60-minute briefing delivered to your inbox every Monday by 9 a.m. local time, covering the state(s) you care about:

  1. Curated bill digest — every state bill that matters to your policy agenda, classified by stage (introduced, in committee, on floor, signed, dead), with a 2-sentence plain-English summary and a "what changed this week" line. No firehose. No alerts on bills you don't care about.
  2. Action priority list — the 3–5 bills where your time will move the most outcome this week, with the specific committee, hearing date, and counter-advocacy you'll be facing.
  3. One drafted advocacy asset per week, written in your org's voice — choose one of: written testimony, an action-alert email to your list, a board memo, a coalition-letter draft, or a one-pager for a legislative office.
  4. A Slack or email thread with me where you can ask follow-ups, request a rewrite, or flag a bill I missed. 24-hour turnaround on questions.

The codebase that powers the digest is internal. You don't log in to anything, configure anything, or learn a new tool. You read an email.

What's required from you

A single 20-minute intake — answered async, in writing — covering:

  • Your top 5 policy priorities for the current session.
  • States you operate in (primary + secondary).
  • Your house style for testimony and action alerts (paste 2–3 examples I'll use as voice training).
  • The contact who'll be the recipient and reviewer.

That's it. No weekly meetings, no project management overhead.

What this is NOT

  • Not a software subscription. You're hiring a service. If you want to log into a dashboard and DIY, FastDemocracy is free and covers all 50 states — go use that, it's good.
  • Not lobbying or political advice. I track bills and draft assets; I don't represent you to legislators or advise on coalition strategy.
  • Not legal advice. Drafts are reviewed by you before they go out.

Why I'm doing this instead of building a SaaS

Three reasons I'll tell you straight:

  1. Plural already serves the SaaS market at $59/month for all 50 states, plus they just got acquired by SAI360 and are drifting to enterprise GRC — that's a price floor I can't beat and don't want to.
  2. FastDemocracy's free tier covers basic alerting for most small orgs. Building a worse version of FastDemocracy is not a business.
  3. What advocacy orgs your size keep telling me they actually need is the write-up — the testimony, the email to the list, the memo to the board. Not another dashboard. That's the part that's hard to automate well and worth paying for.

So I'm building the service, not the software. If 12 months from now it should be software, I'll know — and I'll have 10 customers who tell me what to build.

Kill criteria — for you, not me

  • The drafts I'm producing aren't in your voice well enough that they save you real time.
  • The bill picks are wrong often enough that you're double-checking everything.

You don't have to justify the call. Your time is more valuable than my retention rate.
